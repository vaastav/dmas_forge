# Troubleshooting Dense or Overproofed Loaves

A dense crumb usually comes from one of three causes: weak gluten development, under fermentation, or degassing too aggressively during shaping. If the dough never became airy during bulk fermentation, the loaf often bakes up tight and heavy.

An overproofed dough may feel fragile, sticky, and unable to hold tension. When that happens, a baker can gently reshape it, proof it briefly, and bake it in a loaf tin if free-form structure is lost.

If a loaf spreads instead of springing, reduce proof time slightly, strengthen the dough earlier with additional folds, and make sure the starter is active before mixing.
