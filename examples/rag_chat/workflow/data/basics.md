# Everyday Sourdough Basics

An approachable everyday sourdough loaf works well at 68 to 75 percent hydration. That range is wet enough to create an open, tender crumb but still manageable for bakers who shape by hand.

After mixing the flour, water, salt, and mature starter, many bakers develop strength with stretch-and-folds instead of heavy kneading. Stretch-and-folds build gluten gradually, keep more gas in the dough, and are easier to use with wetter doughs.

Bulk fermentation should continue until the dough feels airy, smoother than when it started, and shows visible expansion. The dough should jiggle slightly when the bowl is shaken.
