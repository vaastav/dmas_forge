# Starter Care

A healthy starter should rise predictably after feeding and smell pleasantly tangy rather than sharply harsh. Warm temperatures speed activity, while cooler kitchens slow fermentation and lengthen the time needed before mixing dough.

If the starter is sluggish, use a few regular feedings at a warm room temperature and wait for it to peak before building the dough. A strong starter improves oven spring and reduces the risk of a gummy interior.
